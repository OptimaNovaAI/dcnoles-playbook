Set the operating rhythm

Your First 30 Days

  1. Set the cadence. Monthly board meetings on a fixed schedule. Treasurer report at every meeting per the bylaws. No surprises.
  2. Stand up the group chat. GroupMe or WhatsApp, per Erin's advice. Someone helps Mitch get set up.
  3. Confirm committee assignments. Members self-selected at orientation. Perry claimed Crystal City hosting alongside Mitch. Lock the rest.
  4. Launch the 12th-seat search. Target: class of 2020 or later, marketing and comms skill set, builds succession behind the marketing lead.
  5. Lock the fall calendar. The football schedule is out. Map watch parties, Seminole Sendoff, and Welcome to the City before September hits.
  6. Plant the non-football flags. First-Thursday networking nights and second-Saturday service projects. Both drew crowds historically and serve the members game watches miss.
